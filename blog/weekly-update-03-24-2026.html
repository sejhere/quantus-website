Quantus Weekly: Second Halving - 4x Faster ZK Proofs in Two Weeks

March 24, 2026  ·  Quantus Team

This week marks a milestone on the ZK proving pipeline - proof generation time has been cut by a factor of four over the past two weeks, driven by a combination of GPU parallelism improvements and a new batching strategy in the aggregation layer.

ZK PROVING PIPELINE

We isolated the bottleneck to witness generation and constraint evaluation in Plonky3. By moving both steps to a CUDA kernel and restructuring the field arithmetic to minimize memory round-trips, single-proof generation dropped from 840ms to 210ms on our reference hardware. This is the second halving in proving time in six months.

The CUDA kernel operates on the Goldilocks field natively, avoiding the expensive modular reduction steps that were previously required when bridging between the CPU and GPU representations. Field elements are packed into 64-bit lanes and processed with coalesced memory access patterns, which is the primary reason the memory round-trip cost dropped so significantly.

AGGREGATION LAYER CHANGES

The aggregation coordinator now processes proofs in rolling batches rather than waiting for a fixed block boundary. This reduces average latency for proof inclusion by 38% under standard load, and keeps proving throughput stable during traffic spikes. We are targeting a 500ms average end-to-end proof time for mainnet.

Rolling batches work by maintaining a sliding window of incoming proofs. When the window fills or a time threshold is reached, whichever comes first, the coordinator triggers aggregation. This ensures that low-traffic periods do not introduce artificial latency from waiting for a full batch, while high-traffic periods still benefit from maximum aggregation efficiency.

WHAT COMES NEXT

Over the next two weeks we plan to open-source the CUDA proving backend and run a final round of adversarial benchmarks against the aggregation layer. The whitepaper will be updated with revised performance benchmarks before the Q2 launch window.

  • Open-source CUDA proving backend release on GitHub
  • Adversarial benchmark suite targeting aggregation layer edge cases
  • Updated whitepaper performance section with new benchmarks
  • Final mainnet readiness review for the ZK subsystem
